About

Albert Belasco was born in London in 1938. On completing his diploma at Woolwich Polytechnic in Pottery and Textiles, he was drafted into the army for compulsory National Service. After receiving compassionate leave to attend to his father who was dying of leukaemia, he began painting obsessively and soon had enough to interest the Portal Gallery in London. The Portal Gallery was established in 1959 by Eric Lister (1926 – 1988) and Lionel Levy (1927 – 2016) and specialised in British figurative artists, often outside the academic tradition such as James Lloyd, John 'Patrick' Byrne and Kit Williams.

This early period of Belasco's work was marked by textural paint application and simple icon-like composition. The 1960s was perhaps his most prolific decade but his style and subject matter quickly settled to iconic or mythic figures, often female and semi naked.
